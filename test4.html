A governance-focused playbook outlines four phases—local validation, stepwise deployment, value measurement, and ongoing surveillance—to move radiology AI from promising models to sustained clinical services. AI EHR Nudge to Prevent Hospital-Acquired Venous Thromboembolism (Protocol)

Predictive Real-World Deployment
Journal: JAMA Network Open

This pragmatic randomized trial will test an artificial intelligence–driven electronic health record nudge that prompts VTE prophylaxis for hospitalized adults at high predicted risk without an active order or documented contraindications. Conducted across urban and rural hospitals, it will compare the AI CDS to standard workflows on hospital-acquired VTE incidence, safety (bleeding), and process outcomes.

Predicting Delayed Clearance in Pediatric High-Dose Methotrexate

Predictive Model Development
Journal: Frontiers in Pharmacology

In 1,485 pediatric high-dose methotrexate cycles, a machine learning model predicted delayed excretion with strong discrimination; XGBoost performed best (AUROC 0.842) and high specificity. An eight-variable model—driven most by serum creatinine and total dose—was released as a web tool for pre-cycle risk stratification to guide supportive care intensity.

September 17, 2025

Rapid RNA Gene Panel Methodology to Aid Antimicrobial Stewardship

Predictive Model Development
Journal: bioRxiv

From RNA-seq of 414 Pseudomonas aeruginosa isolates, 35–40-gene expression panels predicted resistance to meropenem, ciprofloxacin, tobramycin, and ceftazidime with 96–99% test accuracy (F1 0.93–0.99). The compact, largely non-overlapping signatures relied mostly on genes outside current resistance databases, pointing to viable diagnostic panels but needing external validation before clinical use.
